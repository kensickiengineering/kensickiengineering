Brief Biography

Jay Kensicki is a junior chemical engineering student at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. Raised outside of Boston, MA, Jay took a profound interest in scientific & technological development from a young age. Watching the rapid progress Boston was making, particularly in the pharmaceutical industry, sparked his desire to study chemical engineering.


Over time, Jay's interests shifted from R&D to manufacturing: particularly in process design and troubleshooting. Following his sophomore year, Jay interned at Bostik--a large, PSM-regulated plant located in Middleton, MA. This transformative experience reinforced Jay's interests in manufacturing. As a process engineering intern, Jay was responsible for troubleshooting, effective communicating with operators, and, most importantly, completing continuous improvement projects. Over the course of the summer, Jay completed six engineering projects: automating OEE calculations, leading plant safety initiatives, streamlining manufacturing reporting systems, as well as maintaining and updating a large database of P&IDs.


Currently, Jay is at university completing his junior year. He is enrolled in fluid mechanics, kinetics and reactor design, physical chemistry, and other gen-ed courses this semester. Attention to detail and working efficiently are Jay's keys to academic success. As a testament to his discipline, Jay was awarded the Donald F. Othmer Second Year Student Academic Excellence Award for having the highest GPA in his class of chemical engineers. Additionally, Jay is employed by the University as a peer mentor. In this role, Jay is responsible for the health & academic well-being of over 450 freshman.


Jay is also interested in software development. This website was coded by hand using HTML & CSS, and although it is extremely basic, it will continue to be improved over time.
